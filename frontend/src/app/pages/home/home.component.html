About Us

Who is ATAKA Organix?

ATAKA Organix was a joint venture among five agricultural families in the Carolinas with a goal of providing high quality, organic produce in a convenient and affordable way. As young professionals move to the Charlotte area, there has been surging interest in investing in the local economy as well as getting fresh produce. A dramatic poker game-turned midnight business meeting among friends sought to make healthier foods available to the local scene. Some accounts claim Tempestt flipped over the card table after she found out that her favorite local store closed too early to pick up her favorite jam… some up-and-coming brand called “Maher and Hall.” Angelo and Keng felt that they could do better for cheaper, and a friendly rivalry was born. Five years later, we are thriving and now focused on making our superior produce available and convenient for working families, with a focus on extended hours and home delivery. Our vegetables have even received praise and requests for seconds from even the fussiest of toddlers which is easily our proudest accomplishment.

Anziolo Orchard (Black Mountain, NC):

Our 4th generation family has operated out of beautiful Black Mountain, NC just outside of Asheville for the last 98 years. Scenic mountain vistas perfectly serve as the backdrop to our amazing orchards where our Chief arboriculturist, Angelo, has been working to develop a new variety of apples that capture the true essence of fall taking it back from the pumpkin spice clan.

Moua Farm & Orchard (Gaffney, SC)

Summer is always king at Moua Farm and Orchard! We’ve been prized for our award-winning peaches and bringing recognition to the small town off I-85. Fun fact, The Peachoid was inspired by the fruit of our great great grandfather’s first tree and Georgia wishes that their peaches were half as good as ours.

Khandwala Farms (Weddington, NC)

After a breathtaking trip to southeastern France, Aabha wanted to bring some of that culture back to liven up Charlotte’s culinary scene. Our belief is that nothing comes close to our veggies, and our farm-to-table model has the chops to prove it. Our produce is rather well-recognized for both taste and an unusual visual appeal so they are often requested by producers for television cooking shows and foodie bloggers.

Avula Gardens (China Grove, NC)

Our founder Anupama prefers the peace of the small town life, and was inspired to locate her gardens on the banks of Grants Creek after visiting the Pearl Fryar Topiary Garden with her family. While visitors are often awed by the eclectic and imaginative artistry while touring The Garden, everyone knows that the true prize is her renowned Purple Sweet Potato Haupia Pie. The recipe is a closely guarded family secret, but the Avula Purple Sweet potatoes are highly requested, especially for Thanksgiving.

Stormwater Creek Farms (Clinton, NC)

Stormwater Creek Farms is relatively new to the area- founded on land that was severely impacted by flooding of the Williams Old Mill River after a major storm. Our founder, Tempestt, saw potential and began rebuilding back in 2015. Now we are the primary source of tomatoes for much of the Carolina restaurant scene and are expanding operations along the east coast.
